Demo Walkthrough Guide

This guide walks through the user journey of Sarah, a Category Manager, who is preparing a tailored conversational analytics presentation for a client, Fleet Pride (a leading distributor of heavy-duty truck and trailer parts).

1

Act I: Secure Enterprise Authentication

Sarah starts her day. She needs to show the Fleet Pride executive team how easily they can query store locations and parts inventories. She opens the portal and logs in securely using her corporate single sign-on (SSO) credentials.

Figure 1: Sarah's secure single sign-on screen.
2

Act II: The Warm Welcome (Guided Onboarding Tour)

Upon logging in, Sarah is welcomed by a step-by-step guided tour. The walkthrough highlights the settings gear, brand layout configuration, system architecture, and conversational chat input, ensuring a smooth first-time experience.

Figure 2: The guided tour highlighting the configuration controls.
3

Act III: Client-Tailored Customization

Sarah wants the demo portal to match her client's brand. She clicks the Settings icon, enters the Branding Profile tab, searches for the official Fleet Pride branding, and saves. Instantly, all theme colors, SVG headers, and default greeting cards update to align with Fleet Pride's corporate styling.

Figure 3.1: Sarah personalizing the interface with Fleet Pride branding.
Figure 3.2: Customized Home Dashboard branded for Fleet Pride.
4

Act IV: Conversational Business Intelligence

Sarah opens the chat workspace to test the conversational interface. She selects the retail analytics agent and types her question: "What was our total revenue by store location last quarter?". The portal translates her business question, retrieves the live metrics, and returns an interactive bar chart and takeaways.

Figure 4: Chat response rendering the Fleet Pride revenue chart and insights.
5

Act V: Operational Transparency

To demonstrate to the Fleet Pride executives how their queries are securely handled, Sarah clicks Show Architecture in the header. The app displays a clean, visual flowchart showing the secure, end-to-end data pipeline from the browser query to the database, proving enterprise compliance.

Figure 5: High-level visual pipeline diagram.
