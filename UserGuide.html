Demo Mission & Context

This visual guide is designed for GCP Technical Sellers (Customer Engineers / Solution Architects) and Business Sellers (FSRs) to learn how to build, deploy, and walkthrough the Conversational Analytics Showcase. Below, we narrate the user journey of Sarah, a non-technical Category Manager at Tractor Supply Co., exploring the platform for the first time.

1

Act I: Secure Enterprise Authentication

Sarah (our Category Manager) begins her day. She needs to analyze last quarter's revenue across store locations to prepare for an executive meeting. Normally, she would file a ticket with the data team and wait days. Instead, she opens the portal and logs in securely using Google SSO.

⚙️ How it works under the hood: The portal's auth module securely redirects users to Google Identity. If the internal restriction mode is active (RESTRICT_TO_GOOGLE=true), the backend token verifier (auth.py) validates the token and restricts API access strictly to corporate @google.com and Argolis altostrat.com email domains.
Figure 1: Sarah's login screen showing the Google SSO option.
2

Act II: The Warm Welcome (Guided Onboarding Tour)

Upon her first successful login, Sarah is greeted by a fluid, step-by-step guided tour. Rather than throwing her into an empty dashboard, the tour automatically walks her through the settings gear, brand layout configuration, system architecture, and chat input. On mobile viewports, the tooltip dynamically snaps to the bottom center, keeping the UI clean.

Figure 2: Step 1 of the Guided Onboarding Tour highlighting the Settings Gear Icon.
3

Act III: Brand-Tailored Customization

Sarah wants the portal to match her brand identity. She clicks the Settings gear, loads the Branding Profile tab, searches the web for the official Tractor Supply SVG logo, and saves her settings. Instantly, the dashboard theme colors, SVG headers, and default greeting cards update to fit Tractor Supply's styling.

🔒 Restricted Facade: To prevent Google corporate users from hitting permission blocks, we hide the connection settings from non-Argolis users. Googlers and Gmail users bypass credential configs and default to secure Service Account (ADC) mode, while Argolis users retain full User SSO access.
Figure 3.1: Sarah personalizing the logo, greeting text, and styling profile.
Figure 3.2: Customized Home Dashboard aligned with the active brand layout.
4

Act IV: Conversational BI (Natural Language to SQL)

Sarah navigates to the chat workspace. She selects the "Retail Analytics Agent" from the sidebar and toggles on **In-Depth Analysis (Thinking Mode)**. She types her question: "What was our total revenue by store location last quarter?". The reasoning engine translates her question, compiles the SQL, runs it against BigQuery, and returns an interactive Vega chart and structured Insights Card summarizing the trends.

Figure 4: Chat response rendering the Vega revenue bar chart, SQL statement, and executive insights.
5

Act V: Technical Transparency (Show Architecture)

Sarah's Customer Engineer or Solution Architect joins the conversation. To explain how this setup works securely under the hood, they click **Show Architecture** in the header. The app displays an interactive, node-based flowchart mapping out the GCP data orchestrations (BigQuery, Knowledge Catalog, FastAPI/Gemini Reasoning Engine, and React UI).

Figure 5: 4-Node simplified GCP data architecture flow modal.
